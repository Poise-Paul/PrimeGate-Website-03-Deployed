Industry Regulation

Nigeria's real estate industry is governed by a mix of federal and state laws designed to regulate property ownership, transactions, and development. Here are some key regulations shaping the sector:

  1. Home
  2. Industry Regulation

Land Use Act (1978)

This law vests land ownership in state governors, who grant rights of occupancy to individuals and businesses. It also regulates land transactions and ensures government oversight in land allocation.

Nigerian Urban and Regional Planning Act

Governs urban planning, zoning, and development control to ensure orderly growth and prevent illegal structures.

Rent Control and Recovery of Premises Act

Provides guidelines for landlord-tenant relationships, including eviction procedures and rent regulations

Stamp Duty Act

Requires payment of stamp duties on property transactions to validate legal agreements.

Capital Gains Tax Act

Imposes taxes on profits made from property sales

Regulatory Bodies

Agencies like the Federal Ministry of Housing and the Real Estate Developers Association of Nigeria (REDAN) oversee compliance and promote ethical practices in the made from property sales
